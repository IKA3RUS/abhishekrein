# Context

more is one of India's largest supermarket brands with 800 storefronts and ₹6000
crore in annual revenue. In 2022, more launched a grocery delivery app -
quick-commerce players had captured the urban markets with hyperlocal speed.
Behavioural research revealed the real opportunity: more's customers valued
in-store shopping, not delivery speed. What if we met them where they already
were - at our supermarkets?

Research identified key friction points: crowded aisles, finding products, and
checkout queues. Equally important was elevating more+, which had insane
adoption offline but wasn't a highlight of the digital experience. This study
explores how we fundamentally shifted and repositioned more's digital strategy
from standalone delivery to an holistic system that enhanced the in-store
experience.
